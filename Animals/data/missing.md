## Notes

- **Total extant species:** ~2,360 across 511 genera and 35 families (as of Wikipedia's data, sourced from the American Society of Mammalogists and Mammal Species of the World 2005 with molecular updates).
- **38 species** went extinct after 1500 CE (post-Columbian) and are **excluded** from this list. These are found in Capromyidae (8), Echimyidae (6), Cricetidae (11), and Muridae (13).
- Species counts per the largest families: **Muridae** ~801 spp, **Cricetidae** ~694 spp, **Sciuridae** ~284 spp, **Echimyidae** ~88 spp, **Ctenomyidae** ~59 spp.
- Due to the immense number of species, many entries in high-diversity genera (e.g. *Microtus* with 57 spp., *Peromyscus* with 57 spp., *Rattus* with 68 spp., *Mus* with 39 spp.) are noted with a "*(+ additional species)*" marker. The full species within those genera can be found on the respective Wikipedia genus pages.
- Taxonomy follows Wikipedia's List of Rodents, which is based on **Mammal Species of the World (2005)** augmented by molecular phylogenetic studies accepted by the IUCN and American Society of Mammalogists.
